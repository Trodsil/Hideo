My Story

I was born on August 24, 1963, in Setagaya, Tokyo, Japan. As a child, I was fascinated by movies and literature, which inspired me to pursue a career in entertainment. After graduating from college, I joined Konami in 1986 as a game designer.

My first major success came in 1987 when I created the game Metal Gear for the MSX2 computer. The game was a critical and commercial success, and it spawned a series of sequels that made me a household name in the gaming industry.

Over the years, I continued to innovate and push the boundaries of video game storytelling. In 1998, I released Metal Gear Solid for the PlayStation, which is considered a landmark game in the industry for its cinematic storytelling and immersive gameplay.

In 2005, I founded my own studio, Kojima Productions, and went on to create more groundbreaking games, including the critically acclaimed titles Death Stranding and the Metal Gear Solid series.

My work has been recognized with numerous awards and accolades, including induction into the Academy of Interactive Arts and Sciences Hall of Fame in 2016.

In addition to my work in gaming, I am also an avid film buff and have directed and produced several short films and documentaries.

I am known for my attention to detail, complex narratives, and cinematic style. My passion for storytelling and creating immersive experiences for players continues to drive my work in the gaming industry.

Throughout my career, I have always sought to push the boundaries of what is possible in video game storytelling, and I am proud to have influenced an entire generation of game developers with my work.
